Diagrams

This section also includes all the diagrams that we created to help illustrate concepts, relationships, and logical flows of various components of our system.

Diagram 1
Personalized and adaptive learning overview

As a student spends time learning and interacting with learning resources, Actualize analyses interactions and utilizes the student learning model to determine what course resources to provide them with next.

Diagram 2
Traditional learning and personalized and adaptive learning comparison

Students have their own individual learning preferences, some learning modes are more effective while other are inadequate or non-compatible.

In traditional learning students are supplied with, or search for, learning resources that are not always the most appropriate for their needs.

Under personalized and adaptive learning, a learning path can be generated that intelligently identifies the most appropriate learning resources.

Diagram 3
The student model

In order for personalized and adaptive learning to occur, the system collects, analyses, and leverages student data to provide as input to an algorithm that will determine a personalized and adaptive learning path for the student.

Diagram 4
Personalized and adaptive learning algorithm

The algorithm also requires parameters like the Student Learning Objectives (SLO's) to search for the learning resources that will best accommodate the student as they work to reach those goals.

Diagram 5
User actions analyzed by a corresponding intelligent agent or agents

Raw data is made usable through intelligent agents.

Intelligent agents are specialized autonomous programs that operate in a designated environment and process data or events to achieve a single or limited set of goals.

Actualize employs intelligent agents to identify data relevant to the learning preferences of the student.

Diagram 6
Adaptive learning paths

Students can have different learning preferences, as they interact with their lessons and assessments their learning paths evolve according to their preferences, needs, and behaviors.
